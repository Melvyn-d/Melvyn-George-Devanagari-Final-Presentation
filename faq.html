सामान्य प्रश्न FAQ

Technically, it is an Abugida. Unlike a standard alphabet where vowels and consonants have equal status, in Devanagari, consonants carry an inherent vowel that can be changed using diacritic marks.

The standard Devanagari script used for Hindi consists of 11 vowels and 33 consonants, totaling 44 primary characters, though combinations can increase this number.

The Shirorekha is the horizontal line that runs along the top of the characters. It links letters together to form words and gives the script its iconic look.
